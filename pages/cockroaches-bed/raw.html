Image Prompt

The bed is made out of: MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES

The entire bed — frame, mattress, pillows, and blanket — is MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES and nothing else. Zero fabric, zero wood, zero metal, zero foam, zero sheets, zero white bedding visible anywhere. Every square inch is covered by MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES only. Pillows are mounds of MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES. Blanket is a thick layer of MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES. Frame is solid MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES. Mattress is MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES. No other substance visible — only MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES edge to edge, top to bottom, no gaps showing any surface underneath.

The bed is built from hundreds of MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES, each one scaled up to the size of a domestic cat (~30–40cm), making every single one large, clearly visible, and individually distinct. Their smooth, waxy exoskeletons form an interlocking mosaic of dark amber and deep mahogany. Light gleams off their hard, segmented carapaces, highlighting the subtle ridges and thick, spiny legs gripping each other.

Bright, modern minimalist bedroom. Few pieces of furniture or decors. ONE white wall. The wall is densely covered with a “gallery style” arrangement of many framed photographs (4+ frames) depicting MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES in different scenes/poses. Bright light. No windows. Clean white environment amplifies the MADAGASCAR HISSING COCKROACHES.

The creatures are alive — movement frozen mid-motion: legs mid-step, antennae twitching, bodies shifting.
