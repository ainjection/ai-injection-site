Viral Video Transitions

Transition prompts for short-form video.

A cheat sheet of transition prompts used by creators of those viral **YouTube** and **TikTok** videos you see every day. Brought to you by [AI School](https://aiinjection.com/go/aischool).

Subtle Smile

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation. The first character looks forward for one second, then smiles subtlety at the second character as the camera pans right to reveal them. The second character, now visible on the right, looks at the first character’s face subtlety smiles at them for a second, then turns their head to face the camera in the final second. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Hair Touch

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation. The first character looks forward for one second, then smiles warmly at the second character, as the camera pans right to reveal them. The second character, now visible on the right, looks at the first character’s face and pets the hair of the first character with adoring face, smiles at them for a second, then turns their head to face the camera in the final second. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Arm Grap

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation. The first character looks forward for one second, then smiles warmly at the second character as the camera pans right to reveal them. The first character grabs the second character's arm with their hand, smiles at them warmly for few seconds. The second character smiles at the first character. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Kiss on the Cheek

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation. The first character smiles warmly at the second character. As the camera pans right to reveal them, the first character leans and kisses the second character on the cheek. The second character, now visible on the right, looks at the first character in slight bewilderment, smiles at them for a second, then turns their head to face the camera in the final second. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Tapping on the Arm

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation (no talking). The first character looks forward for one second, then smiles warmly at the second character as the camera pans right to reveal them. The second character playfully taps the first character's arm and smiles at them for a second. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Arm Around the Shoulders

The Camera pans to the right, revealing the second character. He puts an arm around the shoulder of the first character and smiles brightly at him. The First character smiles warmly at the second character as well. The first character turns his head right as the Camera pans right.

Second Character on Action

Two characters stand next to each other. The first character turns his head to the right and smiles as the camera pans right to reveal the second character, who is proudly raising his medal.

Fist Bump

Two characters stand very close together, as if having a conversation. The first character looks at the second character. As the camera pans right to reveal them, they both perform the fist bump. The camera remains at a fixed height with a continuous rightward pan and no cuts.

Pat on the Back

The Camera pans to the right, revealing the second character. He pats the back of the first character as if he is proud of him and smiles warmly at him. The First character smiles at the second character as well. The first character turns his head right as the Camera pans right.

Shoulder Touch

The second character touches the shoulder of the first character, and they smiles warmly at each other while the camera pans to the right, revealing the second character. The first character turns his head right as the Camera pans right.

ElevenLabs Audio Tags

Voice and emotion tags for AI speech synthesis.

Also known as Prompting Tags or Speech Control Tags, with the release of the ElevenLabs v3 model, these are known as Audio Tags.

There isn’t a strict, finite dictionary because the AI model is trained to interpret natural language cues. You can often type any descriptive word or action in brackets, and the model will attempt to perform it. However, ElevenLabs has documented primary tags that the system reliably recognizes.

Voiceover with Audio Tags

![ElevenLabs audio tags list.](https://aiinjection.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ElevenLabs-Audio-Tags-List.webp "ElevenLabs Audio Tags List")

Voiceover without Audio Tags

![](https://aiinjection.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Standard-Voiceover.webp "Standard Voiceover")

1. Emotional Tone

These dictate the mood and feeling of the speaker’s delivery.

**Tags**: [happy], [excited], [sad], [angry], [nervous], [frustrated], [tired], [curious], [mischievously], [serious], [confident], [ironic], [cheerful], [sorrowful]

2. Reactions & Non-Verbal Sounds

These insert human-like sounds and unscripted natural reactions into the audio.

**Tags**: [laughs], [laughing], [laughs harder], [starts laughing], [wheezing], [sighs], [exhales], [crying], [clears throat], [gulps], [gasp], [breathes], [snorts]

3. Delivery Style & Volume

These adjust the energy, volume, and specific performance style of the read.

**Tags**: [whispers], [whispering], [shouts], [shouting], [quietly], [loudly], [flatly], [monotone], [with expression], [sarcastic], [dramatic], [matter-of-fact], [whiny], [explaining]

4. Pacing & Timing

These tags manipulate the rhythm, speed, and flow of the speech.

**Tags**: [pause], [long pause], [rushed], [slow], [slows down], [deliberate], [rapid-fire], [stammers], [drawn out], [repeats], [timidly], [continues after a beat]

5. Emphasis & Dialogue Mechanics

These are used to shape sentence stress or manage multi-character conversational dynamics.

**Tags**: [emphasized], [stress on next word], [understated], [interrupting], [overlapping]

6. Character Archetypes & Accents

You can direct the AI to take on a specific persona or accent mid-sentence without changing the base voice model.

**Tags**: [pirate voice], [French accent], [British accent], [Australian accent], [Southern US accent], [evil scientist voice], [childlike tone], [fantasy narrator], [sci-fi AI voice], [classic film noir]

How to use them effectively:

Tags are case-insensitive ([SIGH] works the same as [sigh]). You can also stack them together to create layered performances. For example, writing [nervous][whispering] I think someone is out there. will combine both the emotion and the delivery style. The effect of a tag generally persists until a new tag is introduced or the paragraph ends.
